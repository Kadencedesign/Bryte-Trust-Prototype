  2. "HR Admins can see this" (bubble disclosure, S1 + S4)

  Problem: "This" implies only that specific message. It doesn't convey that the full conversation — including Bryte's responses and any edits — is logged.

  Fix: "Your employer can access this conversation." Or: "This conversation is logged and visible to HR administrators."

  ---
  3. "visible only to you and payroll administrators" (S2 form note)

  Problem: "Only to you" implies a degree of privacy. Legal's concern was exactly this — on a company system, nothing is visible "only" to the employee. The framing creates a false sense of confidentiality.

  Fix: "Visible to you and payroll administrators." Drop "only."

  ---
  4. "Your manager cannot see your conversations" (S2 first-use modal)

  Problem: This may be inaccurate depending on admin role hierarchy. It's also not something we can guarantee without knowing the customer's admin setup. Legal's guidance: don't make specific privacy claims you
   can't back up.

  Fix: Remove this line. The accurate statement is who can see it, not who can't.

  ---
  5. No upfront disclosure in S1 and S3

  Problem: S1 fires immediately — Bryte's first message appears before the user has any chance to know their conversation is visible to their employer. S3 has a coaching banner but no session-level disclosure.

  S2 is the only scenario with a first-use modal. Legal's requirement is that employees know upfront — before they type anything sensitive.

  Fix: Either:
  - Add a lightweight, persistent notice at the top of the chat panel (all scenarios): "Conversations with Bryte are logged and accessible to HR administrators."
  - Or gate S1 and S3 with a first-use acknowledgment similar to S2 — briefer, but present.

  The banner approach is lower friction and consistent across all scenarios without adding another modal.

  ---
  6. Missing: what exactly is stored

  The sprint review flagged this as unresolved — Renae asked about PII visibility for admins, individual vs. aggregate. The current disclosures say conversations are "logged" but don't specify what that
  includes: the user's message, Bryte's response, redacted versions vs. originals, metadata.

  Fix: This is a product decision, not just a copy fix. But the prototype should at minimum be consistent: if redaction happens (S3), the disclosure should note "the conversation visible to administrators
  reflects edits made before processing." For now, flag this as an open question rather than asserting something inaccurate.
